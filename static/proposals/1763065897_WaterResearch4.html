Investment Thesis

Lake Victoria, Africa's largest lake and the world's second-largest freshwater body, is experiencing severe eutrophication driven by:

  • Rapid urbanization with inadequate sanitation infrastructure
  • Direct discharge of nutrient-rich wastewater (N & P)
  • Harmful algal blooms creating hypoxic "dead zones"
  • Devastating impact on fisheries, water security, and public health

This creates a $500M+ annual economic impact across Kenya, Uganda, and Tanzania.

Our approach adapts proven wastewater treatment principles to the African context:

  • Containerized Treatment Units: Modular systems for rapid deployment
  • Nutrient Recovery Technology: Extracting phosphorus and nitrogen as fertilizer
  • Circular Economy Model: Transforming waste into valuable agricultural inputs
  • Appropriate Scale: Targeting peri-urban areas and institutions

This creates both environmental remediation and economic value.

Total Addressable Market: $150M+ across East Africa for decentralized wastewater solutions

Serviceable Available Market: $45M in the Lake Victoria basin alone
